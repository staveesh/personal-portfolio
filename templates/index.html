I am currently a masters student and research assistant at the University of Cape Town, South Africa. My specialization is internet measurements and my research is focussed towards understanding the state of internet in rural communities of South Africa. My research directly benefits these communities by equipping them with a real-time network monitoring and troubleshooting system. Such an addition would make network managers and researchers more aware about internet traffic and help in informing policies and network design changes. The ultimate goal is to provide these underserved communities a resilient and affordable internet access which would increase their overall participation at the global level.

I am highly passionate about building scalable systems that solve complex societal problems. In a past life, I was a software engineer in the finance technology industry. Transitioning from industry to academia at an early stage has been quite a difficult decision for me because I enjoy solving problems which scale to massive groups of users and always have an urge to go back to my CS fundamentals at the same time. So it's kind of like loving the best of both worlds for me. I have built this website as a side project to share my skills and experiences with the tech and academic community. I would love to engage with all of you and hear about your experiences through this website. Hope y'all like it!

My blog posts are going be a crazy mixture of my aha moments, DIY experiences, hiking adventures and much more! One day you may find me working on fixing a nasty bug and the next day I could be learning to play the piano. I am also quite likely to pack my sling bag and run at godspeed towards a (beautiful) Cape Town beach I've never seen before.
